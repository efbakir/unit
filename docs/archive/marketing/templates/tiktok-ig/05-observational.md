# TikTok/IG template — Observational comedy

> **Cadence**: 1/month. **High variance** — flops or hits.
> **Length**: 15-30s vertical.
> **Source**: Source A (DIY) — needs your editorial eye on what's worth zooming on.

---

## Structure

| Time | Action |
|---|---|
| **0-5s** | Deadpan voiceover OR text overlay listing what gym apps now have |
| **5-15s** | Silent zoom on bloated competitor UI screens |
| **15-25s** | Cut to Unit — just log a set, silent, calm |
| **25-30s** | Silence. No CTA. The contrast is the CTA. |

---

## Hook variants (first 5 seconds)

**A** (deadpan list):
> *"Gym apps in 2026: AI coach, social feed, NFT badges, AR scanning, founders-mode, cohort summer."*

**B** (text overlay):
> [Text crawls in: "Hevy now has: AI Coach. Social Feed. Achievements. Goals. Smart Insights. Body Stats. Routines. Templates. Programs. Workouts. Quick Start. Calendar. Streaks. Friends. Discover."]

**C** (quote-style):
> *"The new feature your gym app is launching this week: [insert real recent competitor announcement]"*

**D** (single-word setup):
> [Text: "Bloat."] → cut.

---

## Topic options

| # | Setup | Punch |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | "Hevy onboarding is 14 screens" | Cut to Unit: 3 taps, done |
| 2 | "[Competitor] just added GPT-5 integration" | Cut to Unit: doesn't autocomplete your sets |
| 3 | "AI coach: 'I recommend 5×5 at 75% 1RM today'" | Cut to Unit: ghost-fills last session, athlete decides |
| 4 | "Strava for lifters. Share your squats." | Cut to Unit: no social. No feed. No leaderboard. |
| 5 | "Your gym app's new feature: streaks" | Cut to Unit: no streaks |
| 6 | Multiple competitor screenshots stacked rapid-fire | Cut to Unit: clean, single screen |

---

## Voiceover style (if used)

**Deadpan**, not enthusiastic. Think: bemused craftsman, not stand-up comedian.

```
The new gym apps:
- AI coach.
- Social feed.
- NFT badges.
- AR exercise scanning.
- Founders mode.

I built one that just logs a set.
```

OR: silent. Text overlays carry the joke. Silence + visual contrast is often funnier than narration.

---

## Why this format works

Per `research/viral-patterns-2026-04-29.md` §2d:
> Comedic gym content has a steep "cringe cliff" — observational works (silent zoom on bloated competitor UI), parody/skits don't.

Unit's positioning IS the joke. You don't have to *be* funny — you just have to *be unimpressed*. The contrast does the comedic work.

Also per research: "There's no current dominant indie-fitness creator owning this lane (FitTok is mostly trainers and transformations)." There's an opening here.

## Why we cap at 1/month

High variance. Some posts will hit 100k views, some will get 200. Posting weekly means a lot of low-performance posts dilute the format. Once a month keeps the signal strong.

---

## Risk: the cringe cliff

The line between "observational" and "mocking" is thin. Observational works. Mocking doesn't.

**Observational**: silent zoom on a competitor's bloated screen. Audience reads the contrast.

**Mocking**: voiceover sneering at competitor's choices. Audience reads as bitter.

The brand voice (`PRODUCT.md`: calm, expert, honest) holds the line. If a script starts to feel sneering, cut it.

---

## Recording checklist

- [ ] Screenshots / screen-recordings of 3-5 competitor apps gathered
- [ ] One clean Unit screen-record of a real set being logged
- [ ] Topic chosen — not the same as last month
- [ ] Hook decided (deadpan voiceover, text overlay, or silent contrast)

## Post-production

- [ ] CapCut: rapid-cut competitor screens (5-8s total)
- [ ] Single-take Unit screen-record (10s)
- [ ] No music, OR very subtle ambient bed
- [ ] Submagic captions if voiceover used
- [ ] Closing: silent, on app icon, ~5s

---

## Don't do

- ❌ Mocking specific founders by name
- ❌ Personal attacks on competitor users
- ❌ Sneering tone — stay calm, bemused
- ❌ Cluttered editing — let the visual contrast breathe
- ❌ Music with lyrics — distracts
- ❌ Too long — 30s max. The joke lands or it doesn't; don't drag it.

---

## Punch up at bloat, not at users

The target of the comedy is **product bloat as an industry trend**, not the specific users of competitor apps. People who use Hevy aren't dumb — Hevy was good once. The format mocks the trajectory of the category, not the individuals on it.
