## Why this format works

Per `research/viral-patterns-2026-04-29.md` §3:
> Tony Dinh playbook: ship the demo of the screen, weekly, until one lands. The screen recording + tech-stack mention + real-number screenshot is the X indie-dev breakout pattern.

Each tweet has one purpose. Each has one image. The thread reads as a product narrative, not a launch announcement.

## Pre-post checklist

- [ ] All 5 tweets pre-drafted (use a thread tool like Typefully or just X's native draft)
- [ ] Each tweet ≤ 280 chars
- [ ] Images attached to tweets 1, 2, 3, 4 (not necessarily 5)
- [ ] App Store URL ready in tweet 5
- [ ] Pinned to profile after posting
- [ ] Block 3 hours for replies / quote-tweets

---

## Post-time discipline

- Reply to every quote-tweet within 1 hour
- DMs from the launch tweet are the warm leads — answer them personally
- Don't reply with "thanks!" — reply with one specific thing (an answer to their question, an offer to send a TestFlight code, a follow-up question)

---

## Variants

**Quarterly thread** (W12, W16, W20): same template, update tweets 4-5 with current numbers. Tweet 1 hook becomes:
> "Quarter 1 retrospective on Unit — my indie gym logger. Numbers + lessons."

**Failure-mode thread** (if W8 numbers are bad): same template, but tweet 5 is a question:
> "Open question for indie iOS devs: I'm at $X MRR after 8 weeks. Looks like the bottleneck is [Y]. What would you change?"

---

## Don't do

- ❌ More than 5 tweets in the thread — drops engagement
- ❌ Long bodies on each tweet — tight is shareable
- ❌ Hashtags
- ❌ "Please retweet" calls
- ❌ Skip the dashboard screenshot in tweet 4 — it's the credibility anchor
