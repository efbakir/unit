# X template — pricing philosophy one-liner

> **When**: Week 4-5 of launch, after paywall flips on
> **Format**: single tweet, opinionated
> **Cadence**: post once. If it lands, the W8 recap can reference it; don't post the same thought twice.

---

## The tweet

```
Core logging in Unit will never be paywalled.

Every set you log, every template, every PR detection — free forever.

Pro gates export, Health sync, and custom icons. The lifters who want
those self-select. The lifters who don't, still get the full Gym Test
loop, free.

This is a feature, not a give-away.
```

---

## Variants

**A** (counter-incumbents):
```
Hevy paywalls templates. Strong paywalls weight history. Fitbod paywalls
the AI coach.

Unit's bet: paywall nothing on the path of "log a set under fatigue." Pro
gates the off-path stuff (export, Health sync).

The free tier is the moat.
```

**B** (philosophy):
```
A subscription gym app is asking the lifter to pay for something they
already do. The job is to make the tracking faster than the alternative.

Unit's free forever for set logging. Pro is for the lifters who want
export + Health sync. That's the whole pricing model.
```

**C** (founder commitment):
```
On launch day I promised: core logging in Unit is free forever.

If I ever paywall set logging, every founding member can throw this tweet
in my face.

(Founding members in launch month keep their rate forever, too.)
```

## Why this works

Pricing-philosophy tweets are highly shareable on X among indie devs because they're a stake in the ground. Audiences either agree (and quote-tweet with their own pricing thoughts) or disagree (and quote-tweet with critique). Either way → engagement signal.

Per `research/viral-patterns-2026-04-29.md` §1:
> Underdog framing inverts the "another VC-backed launch" fatigue. A small real number (e.g. $73 MRR) is more shareable than a big abstract one — readers project themselves onto it.

Pricing philosophy + small real numbers = the indie founder's strongest X content.

## Why post this in W4-W5

- Too early (W1-2): no credibility, reads as marketing
- W4-5: paywall has flipped on (per `launch-plan.md`), you have real subs/trial data, the philosophy is being lived
- W6+: it's stale; cover in the W8 recap thread instead

---

## Pre-post checklist

- [ ] Opinion is clear and stake-ground
- [ ] No hedging language ("kind of", "I think", "maybe")
- [ ] No more than one paragraph break
- [ ] No hashtags
- [ ] No link in body — if engagement asks where to download, reply with link

---

## Don't do

- ❌ Soften the take ("most gym apps paywall…" → say *which* apps)
- ❌ Quote your own past tweets — looks self-promotional
- ❌ Tag specific competitor founders — comes across as combative
- ❌ Caps + emoji ("🚨 PRICING THREAD 🚨") — slop signal

---

## Follow-up

If the tweet hits (>100 likes, >5 quote-tweets):
- Reply to each substantive quote-tweet with your reasoning, not just "agree"
- Use it as the basis for a longer Reddit BIP post (`templates/reddit/02-free-tier-philosophy.md`)
- Don't repost the same thought a week later — once it's said, let it stand

If the tweet flops:
- Don't delete
- Don't post a "rethink" — just move on
- Try the same thought as a Reddit post — different audience
