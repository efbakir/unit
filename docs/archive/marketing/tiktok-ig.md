# TikTok / IG Reels playbook

> Five content formats, ranked by realistic install conversion for Unit (zero-friction logger, no coach, no community).
> Screen-on-screen is the unfakeable artifact. Anything that doesn't show your real UI in motion looks like slop, regardless of whether it is.

## The ranking

1. **Tradesman demo** (strongest for Unit)
2. **Founder vlog**
3. **Notebook vs Unit**
4. **Educational micro-lesson**
5. **Observational comedy** (high variance — high upside or flops)

❌ **Transformation (before/after)** — does NOT work for an app. Skip.

---

## Format 1 — Tradesman demo

**Cadence**: 1/week. Cornerstone format.

**Length**: 30-60s vertical.

**Structure**:
- 0-3s: screen-record of a real set being logged with a timer overlay ("1.2s… 1.8s… set logged")
- 3-25s: voiceover narrating what's happening — calm, dry, no music. *"Three taps. Ghost value from last session pre-fills weight and reps. Tap to confirm. Haptic. Done."*
- 25-50s: one specific differentiator from competitors — *"Hevy makes you tap through 4 fields. Strong needs 3. This is one."*
- 50-60s: closing screen with app icon + name + simple CTA

**Hook patterns** (first 3 seconds):
- A visible timer counting up while you log a real set
- Split-screen: bloated competitor onboarding (10 screens) vs Unit (1 screen)
- Cold open with the irritation: hands sweaty, tiny number stepper in competitor app, you fail twice
- Simple text overlay: "Logging a set in 2.4 seconds, one-handed, sweaty"

**CTA**: *"Free on App Store — link in bio."* Pin a comment with the App Store link. Never voice-CTA inside the video (kills retention).

**Why it works**: TikTok algorithm weighs ≥70% completion in first 3s. A focused screen demo with no music + no intro fills that completion target better than any "founder energy" intro.

## Format 2 — Founder vlog

**Cadence**: 1/week.

**Length**: 30-45s vertical.

**Structure**:
- 0-3s: you talking to camera in the gym OR at your desk. *"I quit Hevy 4 months ago because it felt like a spreadsheet under fatigue."*
- 3-25s: 2-3 specific complaints about competitor apps. Be specific — "the set log has 4 taps before haptic", not "it's slow."
- 25-40s: 1 specific thing Unit does instead. Show it on screen.
- 40-45s: invite engagement — *"Show me your tracker setup in comments."*

**Hook patterns**:
- Frustration before solution ("I quit Hevy because…")
- Specific moment ("Last Tuesday in the gym, my 5x5 set timer hit 90 seconds and I still hadn't logged the previous set")
- Brand-cohesive criticism ("The 'AI coach' in [app] is useless under fatigue")

**Why it works**: founder vlogs are the Tony Dinh playbook — ship the demo + you, weekly, until one lands. Audience trusts the human + the screen demo combo.

**Caveats**:
- No "hi guys" or "today I'm going to talk about" — show the screen immediately
- No music
- No transition stings
- Keep it dry — `PRODUCT.md` brand voice: *calm, expert, honest*

## Format 3 — Notebook vs Unit

**Cadence**: 1 every 2 weeks. **Don't burn this out** — it's Unit's defining claim. Overuse turns it into a meme.

**Length**: 20-30s vertical.

**Structure**:
- 0-3s: hand writing in notebook with stopwatch visible
- 3-15s: cut to Unit — ghost-value tap, haptic, done in 2.4s on overlay timer
- 15-25s: side-by-side numbers — notebook 8.1s, Unit 2.4s
- 25-30s: closing — *"Faster than paper. Free on App Store."*

**Hook**: the visible timer + the speed reveal. Specificity > pretty.

**Why it works**: it's the strongest visual proof of the product's defining claim ("faster than paper"). The launch-plan.md §1 explicitly anchors on this comparison.

## Format 4 — Educational micro-lesson

**Cadence**: 1 every 2 weeks.

**Length**: 30-60s vertical.

**Structure**:
- 0-3s: contrarian one-liner. *"Every progression engine is broken. Here's the math."*
- 3-50s: 3-step explanation with screen visuals. Walk through *why* ghost values beat algorithmic recommendations under fatigue.
- 50-60s: closing — *"Unit shows your last session. Never tells you what to lift. Free on App Store."*

**Topic options** (rotate):
- Why ghost values destroy progression engines
- Why "AI coach" recommendations cost you reps
- The math of "seconds per set logged" (your North Star metric)
- Why notebooks beat every gym tracker until 2024
- What "intermediate-to-advanced lifter" actually means in app design

**Why it works**: durable. Educational content has long tail on TikTok — clips can resurface months later. Hooks must be contrarian (not "let me walk you through our features" — that's death).

## Format 5 — Observational comedy

**Cadence**: 1/month. **High variance** — flops or hits.

**Length**: 15-30s vertical.

**Structure**:
- 0-5s: deadpan voice-over OR text overlay listing what gym apps now have
- 5-15s: silent zoom on bloated competitor UI screens
- 15-25s: cut to Unit — just log a set, silent, calm
- 25-30s: silence. No CTA. The contrast is the CTA.

**Topic options**:
- "Gym apps now have AI coaches, social feeds, NFT badges, AR scanning, founders mode, cohort summer" → Unit just logs a set
- "[Hevy/Strong] onboarding is 14 screens" → Unit onboarding is 3 taps
- "[Competitor] just added GPT-5 integration" → Unit doesn't autocomplete your sets

**Why it works**: comedic gym content has a steep "cringe cliff" — observational works (silent zoom, deadpan), parody/skits don't. Don't try to be funny; *be unimpressed.* Unit's positioning IS the joke.

**Risk**: easy to slip into mocking. Stay observational, not mocking. Punch up at bloat, not at users.

## Hooks that work in first 3 seconds (across formats)

- Numbers in the first 3 seconds ("2.4s", "$47", "12 users")
- Frustration before solution ("I quit Hevy", "my paper notebook beat every app")
- Show the screen immediately (no intros, no "today I'm going to")
- Specificity > pretty ("3-second log under fatigue" > "fast and easy")
- Visible timers
- Split-screen comparisons

## CTAs that convert

- "Free on App Store" (not "download now")
- "Link in bio" (not "click below")
- "Show me your setup" (engagement question)
- Silence (the demo is the CTA)

## Hashtags

3-5 max. Use:
- `#lifting` `#gymtok` `#indieapp` `#productivity` `#ios`

Avoid (oversaturated, pure noise):
- `#fitness` `#gym` `#workout` `#fyp` `#viral`

## Cross-posting

- TikTok = primary
- IG Reels = same edits, cross-posted with ≥24h lag (platforms detect simultaneous cross-posts)
- YouTube Shorts = same edits, cross-posted with ≥24h lag (long-tail SEO; 6-month value)
- Skip Threads/X for video — different format expectations

Buffer schedules all three with a staggered offset.

## Realistic install math

A 50k-view TikTok in fitness/productivity-app land typically yields:
- 50-200 App Store impressions
- 5-30 installs

Mid-tail wins (5-20k-view posts that compound) beat lottery-ticket viral attempts. Plan for the engine, not the spike.

## Anti-patterns (codified in `anti-patterns.md`)

- ❌ HeyGen / Synthesia AI talking heads in fitness — shadowban risk in 2025-2026
- ❌ ElevenLabs voice + Pexels stock B-roll combo — slop signature
- ❌ "Day 1238 of solo dev journey" — saturated format
- ❌ Quote-overs of Naval/Levels/founder wisdom
- ❌ Music with lyrics — distracts from screen demo
- ❌ Engagement bait ("comment YES if you agree", "wait for it…")
- ❌ Casting UGC for demographic-clickbait reasons (cast for *authentic lifter signal*)
